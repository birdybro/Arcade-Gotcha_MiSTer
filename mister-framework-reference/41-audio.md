## 1. Purpose & one-line summary

The audio subsystem accepts a stereo 16-bit sample stream from `emu` on `AUDIO_L/R`, runs it through an IIR low-pass filter, a DC blocker, a per-core volume/boost/stereo-mix stage, and emits it on three sinks: I²S to the on-board HDMI chip (which also piggybacks the analog jack and the optional optical/coax path), S/PDIF (TOSLINK), and a stereo sigma-delta DAC that drives the analog jack on single-SDRAM boards. ALSA-from-HPS audio (system beeps, MT32-pi over USB-audio, hybrid cores) is mixed in via a parallel `alsa` module that pulls 48 kHz PCM frames over the f2sdram bridge. A core typically only writes `AUDIO_L`, `AUDIO_R`, `AUDIO_S`, `AUDIO_MIX` — everything downstream is framework-provided.

## 2. The contract (must-obey)

- `AUDIO_L` and `AUDIO_R` are 16-bit framework outputs of `emu`. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/emu_ports.vh:83-84 @ f35083f3b40d)
- `AUDIO_S` is one bit; `AUDIO_S=1` means `AUDIO_L/R` are signed two's-complement 16-bit, `AUDIO_S=0` means unsigned (offset-binary). [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/emu_ports.vh:85 @ f35083f3b40d)
- `AUDIO_MIX[1:0]` selects how much of the opposite channel is folded into each output channel: `0` no mix, `1` 25%, `2` 50%, `3` 100% (mono). [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/emu_ports.vh:86 @ f35083f3b40d)
- `CLK_AUDIO` is an `emu` input pinned at 24.576 MHz. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/emu_ports.vh:82 @ f35083f3b40d)
- `clk_audio` in `sys_top.v` is generated by `pll_audio` from `FPGA_CLK3_50` and is the same clock fed to `emu.CLK_AUDIO`. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/sys_top.v:1569-1577 @ f35083f3b40d)
- `audio_out` samples `core_l`/`core_r` through a 3-cycle stability synchroniser (`cl1`, `cl2`, commit only when `cl1==cl2`); `AUDIO_L/R` must therefore hold stable for at least two `clk_audio` cycles between updates. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:164-174 @ f35083f3b40d)
- Output sample rate is selected by `sample_rate`: `0` = 48 kHz, `1` = 96 kHz; commit-side `sample_ce` divides accordingly. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:30-31, 138-150 @ f35083f3b40d)
- Sign handling inside `audio_out` flips the MSB conditionally on `is_signed`: `{~is_signed ^ cl[15], cl[14:0]}`; declaring `AUDIO_S=0` for samples that are actually signed will flip the sign bit and produce a DC step. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:217-218 @ f35083f3b40d)
- The `AUDIO_MIX` mux blends each channel with `pre_in` (the other channel's pre-attenuation value) using shifts: case 1 subtracts `a2>>3` and adds `pre_in>>2`, case 2 subtracts `a2>>2` and adds `pre_in>>1`, case 3 is full mono sum. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:329-334 @ f35083f3b40d)
- Volume attenuation uses `att[4]` as a mute and `att[3:0]` as a right-shift amount (arithmetic, signed); `att` is driven by `vol_att` from the HPS, default `5'b11111` = mute. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:336-340 @ f35083f3b40d, sys_top.v:317-318 @ f35083f3b40d)
- The IIR filter coefficients (`cx`, `cx0`, `cx1`, `cx2`, `cy0`, `cy1`, `cy2`) and the per-tap filter rate (`flt_rate`) are runtime-loaded from the HPS over SPI; defaults on power-up correspond to a 7.056 MHz 3-tap LPF (`LPF20000.txt`). [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/sys_top.v:339-346, 387-394, 477-491 @ f35083f3b40d)
- The DC blocker is always in series after the IIR filter and before the mixer — it cannot be bypassed by the core. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:223-243 @ f35083f3b40d)
- I²S framing uses `AUDIO_DW=16`; data is shifted MSB-first per channel, `lrclk` flips after every 16-bit slot, latch on `lrclk` rising marks new sample. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/i2s.v:2-5, 36-48 @ f35083f3b40d)
- S/PDIF is 16-bit-per-subframe, 192 frames per audio block, BMC-encoded; `bit_out_en_i` is a single-cycle pulse at the bit rate (48 kHz × 32 × 2 × 2 = 6.144 MHz for 48 kHz audio). [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/spdif.v:41-51, 78-87 @ f35083f3b40d)
- Sigma-delta DAC is instantiated with `MSBI=15` (16-bit input) and is fed offset-binary `{~al[15], al[14:0]}` — i.e. the framework converts the signed mixer output to offset-binary by flipping the sign bit before the DAC. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:122-136 @ f35083f3b40d, sigma_delta_dac.v:6-12 @ f35083f3b40d)
- ALSA is gated by `MISTER_DISABLE_ALSA`; when present, `alsa_l/alsa_r` feed `audio_out` as `linux_audio` and are summed with the boosted core samples in `aud_mix_top` (`a2 <= {a1[15],a1} + {linux_audio[15],linux_audio}`). [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/sys_top.v:1603-1606, 1619-1657 @ f35083f3b40d, audio_out.sv:324-325 @ f35083f3b40d)
- The HDMI piggyback contract: `HDMI_MCLK = clk_audio`, `HDMI_SCLK/LRCLK/I2S` are the I²S outputs of `audio_out`; on single-SDRAM boards the analog `AUDIO_L/R/SPDIF` jack pins are switched between the sigma-delta DACs (default) and the I²S lines via `SW[0]` / `mcp_en`. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/sys_top.v:1564-1577 @ f35083f3b40d)
- `audio_mix` (the 2-bit OSD-derived mix selector) is an `emu` output; the framework does not generate it — the core decides whether to expose the option. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/emu_ports.vh:86 @ f35083f3b40d, sys_top.v:1683, 1819 @ f35083f3b40d)
- HPS-side volume command is `UIO_AUDVOL (0x26)`, filter command is `UIO_SET_AFILTER (0x39)`; both are issued by `Main_MiSTer/audio.cpp`. [C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Main_MiSTer/audio.cpp:34, 122, 136 @ 136737b4bed4)

## 4. Sequencing & timing

### 4.1 Datapath order (always-on)

```
emu.AUDIO_L/R[15:0]  -->  cl/cr (3-cycle stability sync)
   AUDIO_S          -->  sign XOR {~is_signed ^ cl[15], cl[14:0]}
                    -->  IIR_filter (3-tap, time-multiplexed L/R, ce = flt_ce*2)
                    -->  DC_blocker (sample_ce paced, mutes during ramp)
                    -->  aud_mix_top (boost -> + alsa_l/r -> AUDIO_MIX blend -> att shift -> clamp)
                                                 ^
                                          alsa.pcm_l/r (signed 48 kHz)
                    -->  al/ar (final 16-bit signed)
                          |--> i2s (HDMI_SCLK/LRCLK/I2S)
                          |--> spdif (AUDIO_SPDIF jack / TOSLINK)
                          |--> sigma_delta_dac (analog L/R jack, offset-binary)
```

### 4.2 Clock-enable cadence (`CLK_RATE` = 24,576,000 Hz)

`audio_out` derives three CE pulses from `clk` (24.576 MHz):

| CE | Default rate | Formula | Used by |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `mclk_ce` | 12.288 MHz @ 48 kHz, 24.576 MHz @ 96 kHz | DDA: `cnt += real_ce`, fire on `cnt >= CLK_RATE`; `real_ce = sample_rate ? CE_RATE*2 : CE_RATE`, `CE_RATE = 48000*16*8` | `spdif.bit_out_en_i`, `i2s_ce` divider |
| `i2s_ce` | 6.144 MHz @ 48 kHz | `mclk_ce` divided by 2 (`div ^= 1`) | `i2s.ce` |
| `sample_ce` | 48 kHz / 96 kHz | `div` accumulator with `1 << sample_rate` step | DC blocker, mixer commit |
| `flt_ce` | `flt_rate` Hz (default 7.056 MHz) | DDA: `cnt += {flt_rate, 1'b0}`, fire on `cnt >= CLK_RATE` (×2 because IIR multiplexes L/R) | IIR filter `ce` |

[C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:66-95, 138-162 @ f35083f3b40d)

### 4.3 Stability synchroniser on AUDIO_L/R

```
clk_audio    |‾|_|‾|_|‾|_|‾|_|‾|_|‾|_|‾|_
core_l[15:0] ── X ──── Y ──── Y ──── Y ────  (core toggles per sample at ~CLK_AUDIO/512)
cl1          ── X' ─── Y' ─── Y' ─── Y' ───  (delayed 1 cycle)
cl2          ──── X'' ── Y'' ── Y'' ── Y'' ─ (delayed 2 cycles)
cl           ──── X ──── Y ──── Y ──── Y ─── (commits when cl1==cl2)
                  ^ core_l must remain stable ≥2 clk cycles
```

If `core_l` changes every cycle (faster than `audio_out` can latch), `cl1` and `cl2` never agree and `cl` never updates → silence.

[C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:164-174 @ f35083f3b40d)

### 4.4 I²S frame (16-bit, AUDIO_DW = 16)

```
i2s_ce      _|‾|_|‾|_|‾|_|‾|_|‾|_|‾|_  ... (6.144 MHz pulses)
msclk       _____|‾‾‾‾|____|‾‾‾‾|____  ... (toggled by ce; sclk = msclk)
sclk        _____|‾‾‾‾|____|‾‾‾‾|____  ... (1.536 MHz @ 16 bits × 48 kHz × 2)
lrclk       |‾‾‾‾ 16 bits left ‾‾‾‾|____ 16 bits right ____|‾‾‾‾ left ...
sdata       ─ L15 L14 ... L1 L0  R15 R14 ... R1 R0  L15 ...
                                                ^ latch new sample on lrclk rising
```

Bit shifted out on `msclk` high (the "second half" of each `i2s_ce`); `sdata` MSB-first; `left/right` registers loaded only when `lrclk` rises (i.e. left half ends).

[C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/i2s.v:19-52 @ f35083f3b40d)

### 4.5 S/PDIF frame

- 32 timeslots per subframe × 2 BMC half-bits = 64 bit-times per subframe.
- 2 subframes per frame (L then R), 192 frames per audio block (384 subframes).
- Preambles: Z(B) = `00010111` at subframe 0 (start of block, left), Y(W) for right, X(M) for non-block-start left.
- Channel-status bits stamp 48 kHz at subframe pair 25 (bit pattern `0100` across frames 24-27 indicates 48 kHz).
- BMC encoding: each timeslot lasts two `bit_out_en_i` pulses; the encoder toggles in the middle of every slot, and additionally at the start of slots that carry a `1`.

[C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/spdif.v:78-87, 129-183, 257-310 @ f35083f3b40d)

### 4.6 ALSA flow

```
HPS (Main_MiSTer) writes PCM into HPS-side DDR3 ring buffer
   |
   v  cyclonev_hps_interface_peripheral_spi_master (sys_top.v:1621-1629)
   v
alsa.sv SPI slave latches buf_info {buf_wptr, buf_len, buf_addr}
   |
   v  Every ce_sample (48 kHz + hurryup ramp)
   v
alsa.sv issues ram_req with ram_address = buf_addr + buf_rptr
   |
   v  f2sdram returns 64 bits = 2 stereo 16+16 frames
   v
alsa.sv shifts out {pcm_r, pcm_l} into audio_out.alsa_l/alsa_r
```

`hurryup` (0..4) is reported back to the HPS over SPI MISO so the HPS can adjust its write cadence; large queue → larger `hurryup` → faster `ce_sample` accumulator step (acc += 48000 + {hurryup,6'd0}).

[C] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/alsa.sv:44-156 @ f35083f3b40d)

### 4.7 HPS-side audio commands (`Main_MiSTer/audio.cpp`)

- `UIO_AUDVOL (0x26)` — single byte, sent on every `send_volume()`; lower 3 bits = right-shift count, bit 4 = mute, bits 5-6 = boost. Maps to `vol_att`/`vol_boost` in `sys_top.v:466, 477`.
- `UIO_SET_AFILTER (0x39)` — multi-word; first word includes `{vol_boost, acx_att}`, then `flt_rate` (32-bit), `cx` (40-bit), `cx0/1/2` (8-bit each), `cy0/1/2` (24-bit each). Parsed line-by-line out of an `*.txt` filter file.

- Mono cores wire `AUDIO_L == AUDIO_R` and set `AUDIO_MIX = 0`. The framework still ships both channels through `audio_out`; users gain nothing from `AUDIO_MIX` in this configuration. [V] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/Template.sv:42-44 @ f35083f3b40d)
- Unsigned-DAC cores (typical of PSG/AY/SAA1099 generators that produce offset-binary natively) set `AUDIO_S = 0`. The framework XORs the MSB on the IIR input path to convert to signed before filtering. [V] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:217-218 @ f35083f3b40d)
- The IIR filter coefficients default to the contents of `LPF20000.txt` (7.056 MHz, 3-tap, ≈20 kHz Fc). Cores that pre-filter on their own DSP-rate clock often ship an `*_afilter.cfg` that selects a different `*.txt` from `/media/fat/filters/audio/` so the framework filter is a softer LPF. [I] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/sys_top.v:339-346 @ f35083f3b40d, archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Main_MiSTer/LPF20000.txt:1-27 @ 136737b4bed4, audio.cpp:39-119 @ 136737b4bed4)
- Chiptune cores generating raw square waves at the framework rate **must** rely on the framework IIR LPF — they have no internal anti-imaging filter, and the bare 16-bit samples will alias horribly on TOSLINK/HDMI sinks otherwise. The 3-tap `LPF20000.txt` is calibrated for that case. [I] (archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/iir_filter.v:22-52 @ f35083f3b40d, LPF20000.txt:1-27 @ 136737b4bed4)

## 7. Anti-patterns

### A.1 `AUDIO_S = 0` with signed two's-complement samples

- **Symptom:** Silence on power-up replaced by a loud thump that decays over ~1-2 seconds, then heavy DC bias on quiet passages. Headphones may pop. The DC_blocker eventually settles.
- **Cause:** `audio_out.sv:217-218` does `{~is_signed ^ cl[15], cl[14:0]}`. With `is_signed=0` and a signed core sample, the MSB is XOR-inverted: `0x0000` becomes `0x8000` (−32768), turning silence into the negative rail.
- **Fix:** Set `AUDIO_S = 1` if the core's samples are signed two's-complement (the modern convention). Otherwise convert your samples to offset-binary (`sample + 16'h8000`) and keep `AUDIO_S = 0`.
- **Citation:** archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:217-218 @ f35083f3b40d

### A.2 Updating `AUDIO_L/R` on every `CLK_AUDIO` edge

- **Symptom:** Total silence on I²S, S/PDIF, and analog jacks even though the core's internal sample counter is incrementing.
- **Cause:** `audio_out` waits for `cl1 == cl2` (two consecutive `clk` cycles with the same value) before committing `cl <= cl2`. If the core feeds new samples on every `CLK_AUDIO` edge (e.g. by combinationally routing a free-running DSP at `clk_audio` to `AUDIO_L`), the synchroniser never sees agreement.
- **Fix:** Drive `AUDIO_L/R` from a register that updates only on the core's sample-rate clock enable (typically 44.1/48 kHz, or your DSP rate divided down). Hold the previous value between updates.
- **Citation:** archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:164-174 @ f35083f3b40d

### A.3 Hardcoding `AUDIO_L = AUDIO_R` and ignoring `AUDIO_MIX`

- **Symptom:** Stereo cores sound spatially correct but users with mono speakers/headphones complain that some content vanishes; OSD mix option has no effect.
- **Cause:** Tying both channels to one stream defeats `aud_mix_top`'s cross-channel blender — there is no opposite-channel content for case 1/2/3 to fold in, so the option becomes a no-op.
- **Fix:** Feed real per-channel samples on `AUDIO_L`/`AUDIO_R`, expose `AUDIO_MIX` from a 2-bit OSD option (`O[bit:bit]`), and let `aud_mix_top` perform the blend.
- **Citation:** archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/audio_out.sv:281-341 @ f35083f3b40d

### A.4 Skipping the framework filter for chiptune cores

- **Symptom:** S/PDIF and HDMI audio sound buzzy with stair-step artefacts above ~10 kHz; analog jack sounds harsher than expected.
- **Cause:** Square-wave generators output values that change instantaneously at the core's sample rate. Without the framework's 3-tap IIR LPF (default `LPF20000.txt`, ≈20 kHz Fc), images of the sample rate alias into the audio band when the chain upsamples to 48/96 kHz.
- **Fix:** Do not bypass the framework filter. If a custom curve is desired, ship a `*_afilter.cfg` and a matching `*.txt` (format per `LPF20000.txt`) so the HPS loads coefficients into `acx/acy*` over `UIO_SET_AFILTER`.
- **Citation:** archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/iir_filter.v:22-52 @ f35083f3b40d, archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Main_MiSTer/LPF20000.txt:1-27 @ 136737b4bed4, archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Main_MiSTer/audio.cpp:39-119 @ 136737b4bed4

### A.5 Forgetting the `MISTER_DISABLE_ALSA` gate

- **Symptom:** Build succeeds but ALSA-from-HPS audio (system bell, MT32-pi over USB-audio, web-radio plugins) is silent; some boards report fitter errors on `alsa_l/alsa_r` nets.
- **Cause:** The `alsa` module and the SPI master in `sys_top.v:1619-1657` are inside `` `ifndef MISTER_DISABLE_ALSA ``. If a core forces the macro on for an unrelated reason, those nets are floating.
- **Fix:** Only set `MISTER_DISABLE_ALSA` when intentionally disabling Linux audio (e.g. dual-SDRAM cores that need the SPI master for something else).
- **Citation:** archive/github/MiSTer-devel/Template_MiSTer/sys/sys_top.v:1603-1657 @ f35083f3b40d

## 8. Verification

- **OSD audio menu** — change Volume in the OSD and confirm `set_volume()` decrements `vol_att` (visible as audible step changes in 6 dB increments).
- **Audio filter file** — copy `LPF20000.txt` into `/media/fat/filters/audio/`, pick it in the OSD's *Audio Filter* submenu; the framework reports `Loading audio filter: ...` over the serial console (`audio.cpp:40`). Power-up defaults already match this file.
- **Sign-bit smoke test** — toggle `AUDIO_S` while playing a 1 kHz sine in the core; the wrong polarity produces a 32 kHz buzz instead of the sine. The DC_blocker's `mute` (asserted while `dly2` ramps up) hides this for the first ~340 ms — wait past that.
- **96 kHz mode** — flip `cfg[6]` (OSD *Output Sample Rate*) and verify the I²S bit-clock period doubles (use a logic analyser on `HDMI_SCLK`).
- **ALSA mixing** — run `aplay /dev/zero` on the HPS; if `alsa_l/r` are connected, the audio jack will not produce noise but the alsa FSM's `hurryup` MISO byte (visible on the SPI slave) will increment.
- **S/PDIF sanity** — a TOSLINK receiver should lock and report 48 kHz; if it reports 44.1 kHz the channel-status bits in `spdif.v:175-182` were modified or the `mclk_ce` accumulator constant is wrong (verify `CLK_RATE = 24_576_000`).
- **DAC polarity** — `sigma_delta_dac` has parameter `INV=1`; if the analog jack is dead but I²S works, suspect a board-revision wiring inversion or the `{~al[15], al[14:0]}` offset-binary conversion was bypassed.
- **`MiSTer.ini` audio-related flags** — `volumectl_*`, `direct_video` (changes audio routing when piggyback goes to USER I/O via `SW[1]`).
- **Cross-link** — for `clk_audio` PLL settings, see `12-clocks-resets-plls.md`; for HDMI audio path (chip-side I²C config), see `40-video.md`.
